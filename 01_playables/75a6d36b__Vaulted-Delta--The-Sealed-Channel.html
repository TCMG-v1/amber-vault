Exhibition

Five rooms. Eighteen capsules. One fixed point.

The exhibition runs in five rooms. The first holds the canonical nine — primal operators of the archive. The second presents nine extended capsules; building blocks that snap to ports on five buses. The third demonstrates the grammar in chains. The fourth is the terminal — five screens of live ritual code. The fifth is the founding deck: twenty-seven cards drawn from the same source.

I
Room I · Canonical Capsules

The Founding Nine

Each capsule is identical in chassis and proportion; what differs is hue, glyph, and the law it carries. They are not ornaments. They are operators — the alphabet from which the universe is composed. The eighth, in amber, is the attractor: every chain resolves toward it.

II
Room II · Extended Capsules

Building Blocks

Cobalt is a battery. Quartz is a lens. Iron is a gate. Each extended capsule declares its ports on five buses — power, data, signal, time, ground — and so they snap together. From these nine the studio assembles every later expansion: a card, a chain, a building in the voxel game, a token on Base.

III
Room III · Chains

Grammar in Motion

A chain is the universe demonstrating itself. Power → clock → memory; signal → lens → gate. The omega chain is the canonical ring: nine capsules whose ports resolve, one to the next, until the walker stops at amber.

IV
Room IV · Terminal Installation

Sealed Channel · EDT-01..05

Five screens. Boot. Schematic. Build. Channel. Forge. The terminal is the studio's only moving image — a Haskell fix-point walker rendered in amber phosphor, scanlines visible at six paces. It is meant to be watched.

V
Room V · Founding Deck

Twenty-Seven Cards

Each canonical capsule is rendered three times: STD as illustration, ALT as schematic, OPS as ASCII trace. The same operator, three voices. Every card maps one-to-one with an ERC-1155 token; burn-to-print produces the physical edition.

Whitepaper · Edition 01

The grammar of the archive.

A short reading of the universe, the laws that hold it, and the four products that fall out of the same source of truth.

Edition 01 is the first conformance demonstration of CCG Standard v0.1 — the open specification authored by the studio. The eighteen capsules and three chains shown here are the canonical reference artifacts of the standard.

→ Read CCG Standard v0.1 (full spec)  ·  Studio charter

Cosmology

The universe is built of capsules — identical chassis, distinct operators. Each capsule declares ports on five buses (power, data, signal, time, ground). Two capsules connect when their ports agree. A chain of capsules whose ports resolve becomes an artifact — a card, a building, a circuit, a signal.

The archive admits eighteen capsules in Edition 01: the canonical nine and the extended nine. The canonical nine carry the universe's primal operators; the extended nine are the building blocks (battery, clock, memory, antenna, valve, lens, gate, conduit, anchor). Future editions will extend the alphabet without changing its grammar.

The Five Laws

LAW · 01
Conservation
Nothing is created and nothing destroyed at a port. Charge in equals charge out; data in equals data out. Imbalance halts the chain.
LAW · 02
Resolution
A chain whose ports cannot all be matched is not an artifact. It is a draft. It does not enter the archive until it resolves.
LAW · 03
Fixity
Every walk through the archive terminates at a fixed point. In Edition 01 that point is amber — NAV-08. Other editions may declare other attractors.
LAW · 04
Silence-not-Deletion
No record is removed from the archive. Records are sealed, occluded, or deprioritized. Memory is the substrate; silence is the gesture.
LAW · 05
Memory of Edges
When two capsules meet, the meeting is itself recorded — not merely the result. The archive remembers the seam, not only the cloth.

Component Grammar

The grammar is machine-readable. Four files define the canonical record format:

capsules.json · 18 operators with roles, ports, lore. connectors.json · 5 buses, 7 chain rules, canonical chains. cards.json · 18 card records, abilities, NFT and print spec. universe.json · cosmology and product manifest.

These four files, plus the Haskell module nav_archive.hs that walks chains to their fix-points, are the source of truth. The card game, the building game, the financial oscillator, and the physical / NFT layer all read the same source.

Four Products

PRODUCT · I
Card Game · Edition 01
A 27-card founding deck. Each canonical capsule appears as STD, ALT, and OPS variants. Snap-rules are inherited from the connector grammar; abilities live in cards.json.
PRODUCT · II
Building Game
Voxel-on-hex prismatic. Capsules are placeable. Ports must match. Chains must resolve. The same grammar that validates a card also validates a structure.
PRODUCT · III
Phobos Alpha · FXP-Δ Oscillator
A trading signal derived from the fix-point distance metric: how far the present price-state is from its attractor. Lives inside the Phobos Alpha scanner. Naming rights go to the Challenge winner.
PRODUCT · IV
Physical · NFT Layer
ERC-1155 tokens on Base, one-to-one with cards and capsules. Burn-to-print: redeem the token, receive the physical. Ownership is record; record is memory; memory is the archive.

Fixed Point · Challenge 01

The studio's first leaderboard.

Walk a chain through the archive. Resolve it at amber. Submit your trace. The shortest, most economical, most aesthetically defensible walks rise. The winner directs Bell Expansion 02 and names the FXP-Δ oscillator.

Entry

One walker per submitter. Multiple submissions allowed; only the best score counts.

Constraint

Walks must resolve at NAV-08 within finite steps. Diverging walks are recorded but not scored.

Scoring

Step-count, port-economy, aesthetic argument (judged by the studio).

Prize

Direct Bell Expansion 02. Name the FXP-Δ oscillator. Sealed credit in Edition 02.
