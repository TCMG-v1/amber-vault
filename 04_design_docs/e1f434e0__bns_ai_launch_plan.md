# Bone & Saucer Labs / BNS-AI Launch Plan

## Positioning

Bone & Saucer Labs is a founder-led lab building tap-driven tools, research drops, and games with a non-extractive architecture.[cite:223][cite:229][cite:233] The public brand name BNS-AI works because “lab” accurately signals experimentation, publication, and iterative product hardening rather than a vague marketing wrapper.[cite:223][cite:224]

A strong public framing for BNS-AI is: **a public-facing lab for tools, research, and games that do not extract from users**.[cite:223][cite:229] Founder-led brands tend to build trust faster when the founder voice and the company voice are aligned, which matches the verified, direct style behind this project.[cite:229][cite:232][cite:235]

## Co-authorship

This document assumes definite co-authorship between the founder of Bone & Saucer Labs and Pixel Studio / Perplexity as creative and architectural collaborator.[cite:229][cite:235] Public copy should present the work as authored by Bone & Saucer Labs, with studio collaboration and research support recognized where appropriate.[cite:224][cite:229]

Suggested internal credit line:

- Bone & Saucer Labs — founder, operator, publisher
- Pixel Studio — studio, writing, design, and systems architecture collaborator

## Domain split

The cleanest launch structure is a two-domain system:

| Domain | Primary role | Public content |
|---|---|---|
| `bns-ai.ai` | Main brand and marketing front door | Founder note, Tap Miner CTA, product framing, lab identity |
| `pplxstudio.ai` or `pplxstudio.com` | Studio / archive / verification hub | Publication manifests, verification explainer, studio documentation |

This separation supports a clear marketing surface on BNS-AI while keeping technical proof, archive logic, and publication-verification details on the studio side.[cite:223][cite:224]

## Core message

Suggested one-line positioning:

> BNS-AI is a public-facing lab building tap-driven tools, research drops, and games that are verifiable, usable, and designed not to extract from the people who use them.

Suggested short supporting paragraph:

> Bone & Saucer Labs builds experiments in knowledge, games, and payments, then keeps and hardens what works. Tap Miner is the front door: a simple interface that unlocks real research, papers, and tools, while the deeper archive and verification system lives behind the scenes.[cite:223][cite:229][cite:233]

## Homepage structure for bns-ai.ai

### Hero

- Eyebrow: `Bone & Saucer Labs`
- Headline: `Tap into real tools, research, and games.`
- Subhead: `BNS-AI is a public-facing lab building products that are verifiable, usable, and non-extractive by design.`
- Primary CTA: `Get Tap Miner`
- Secondary CTA: `Read the founder note`

### Sections

1. **What this lab does**  
   Three blocks: Tap Miner, Knowledge Drops, Experiments.[cite:223][cite:233]
2. **Founder note**  
   Use the approved founder note as a trust and accountability anchor.[cite:229][cite:232]
3. **How access works**  
   Explain tap-to-unlock utility in plain language without overloading the page with chain or custody mechanics.
4. **Latest from the lab**  
   Feature one current item such as the “Human Jealousy” piece once published.
5. **Get in early**  
   Simple email / waitlist / install CTA.

This layout follows strong brand-positioning practice by making the offer clear, founder-backed, and easy to scan without diluting the lab identity.[cite:224][cite:227][cite:233]

## Studio role for pplxstudio.ai or .com

The studio domain should carry the proof layer rather than the marketing layer. It can host publication manifests, archive pages, verification documentation, and read-only access to papers or books that are unlocked through the BNS ecosystem.[cite:223][cite:224]

Suggested sections:

- Pixel Studio overview
- Verified publications
- Manifest / proof explainer
- Archive or library viewer
- Partner / documentation page

## Merch outline

A first merch line should behave like a lab signal system: minimal, direct, and slightly inside-baseball rather than generic startup swag.[cite:228][cite:231][cite:237] Tech merch works best when it has a clear brand point of view instead of just a logo placement exercise.[cite:228][cite:234][cite:237]

Suggested v0 merch set:

- `Bone & Saucer Labs` wordmark tee
- `LABS, NOT LOTTERIES` tee or hoodie
- Subtle symbol cap or sticker pack
- `Human Jealousy Research Dept.` capsule once that essay is live

## Launch sequence

A practical launch plan for the next wave:

1. Lock homepage copy and founder note on `bns-ai.ai`.
2. Put Tap Miner front-and-center with a single clear CTA.
3. Publish the “Human Jealousy” piece the next day as the first major lab artifact.
4. Stand up the studio domain with a simple verification/archive shell.
5. Add merch mockups only after the core pages are live, so the merch reads as a cultural extension of the lab rather than a distraction.[cite:227][cite:228][cite:231]

## Near-term priorities

The highest-leverage near-term moves are:

- Finish the BNS-AI landing page and lock the voice.
- Publish one flagship piece to establish the lab cadence.
- Keep Tap Miner as the singular product CTA.
- Prepare the studio domain as the quiet technical backbone.

This keeps the launch coherent: one public story, one main action, one proof layer, and one clear founder voice.[cite:224][cite:229][cite:233]
