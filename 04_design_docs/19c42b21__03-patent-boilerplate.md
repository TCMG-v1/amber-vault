## 1. Recitals & Purpose

This document is the **master patent boilerplate** for the inventions identified in Schedule A (collectively, the "Inventions"). It is intended to:

(a) memorialize, for each Invention, the inventor(s), conception date, reduction-to-practice date (actual or constructive), and the entity to which patent rights have been or shall be assigned;
(b) preserve a single, signed record sufficient to support U.S. and foreign filings, including provisional, non-provisional, design, plant, and PCT applications;
(c) document the prior-art search posture, freedom-to-operate considerations, and inventor's contribution narrative for each Invention;
(d) establish the recordation, maintenance, and prosecution-correspondence protocols; and
(e) coordinate the patent stack with the Trademark Boilerplate (Document 01) and Copyright Boilerplate (Document 02) for inventions that overlap multiple regimes (e.g., software inventions that also embody copyrighted code and trademarked brand elements).

## 2. Definitions

- **"Invention"** — any patentable subject matter conceived by the Inventor, whether or not yet reduced to practice, identified in Schedule A.
- **"Conception"** — the formation in the mind of the Inventor of a definite and permanent idea of the complete and operative Invention, sufficient to enable a person of ordinary skill in the relevant art to reduce it to practice without undue experimentation.
- **"Reduction to Practice"** — either (i) actual reduction to practice (a working embodiment of the Invention) or (ii) constructive reduction to practice (filing of a patent application that adequately discloses the Invention under 35 U.S.C. § 112).
- **"Provisional Application"** — a U.S. provisional patent application filed under 35 U.S.C. § 111(b), establishing a priority date for twelve (12) months.
- **"Non-Provisional Application"** — a U.S. utility patent application filed under 35 U.S.C. § 111(a).
- **"PCT Application"** — an international application under the Patent Cooperation Treaty.
- **"Assignee"** — the Owner Entity to which the Inventor has assigned, or shall assign, the patent rights.
- **"Lab Notebook"** — the contemporaneous, dated, signed (and ideally witnessed) record of the Inventor's work on the Invention, retained as evidence of conception and diligence.

## 5. Pre-Filing Hygiene

For each Invention, before filing, the Inventor and counsel shall:

(a) **Confidentiality.** Treat the Invention as confidential and disclose only under NDA or to attorneys/agents subject to professional duties of confidentiality.

(b) **Prior-Art Search.** Conduct a reasonable search of issued patents, published applications, scholarly literature, and commercial products, documenting the results in the matter file.

(c) **Public Disclosure Audit.** Inventory all prior public disclosures of the Invention by the Inventor (presentations, posts, GitHub commits, redink.adult publications, livestreams). Under 35 U.S.C. § 102(b)(1), the Inventor enjoys a **one-year grace period** in the U.S. from the Inventor's own public disclosure, BUT this grace period is **not honored in most foreign jurisdictions** (Europe, China, Japan are absolute-novelty regimes). File before public disclosure when foreign rights are desired.

(d) **On-Sale Bar.** Confirm no offer-for-sale of an embodiment of the Invention more than one (1) year before the U.S. priority date.

(e) **Inventorship Determination.** Confirm that only those who made an inventive contribution to at least one claim of the eventual application are named as inventors; over-inclusion and under-inclusion both threaten validity under 35 U.S.C. § 256.

(f) **Freedom-to-Operate.** Where commercial practice of the Invention is intended, conduct a freedom-to-operate review separate from the patentability search.

## 6. Filing Strategy

The default filing strategy for each Invention shall be:

1. **Provisional U.S. Application** filed promptly after disclosure to establish a priority date at low cost. The provisional must include sufficient written description and enablement under 35 U.S.C. § 112 to support the claims of any later non-provisional.

2. **Non-Provisional U.S. Application** filed within twelve (12) months of the provisional, claiming priority, with full claim set.

3. **PCT Application** filed within the same twelve-month window if international rights are sought, designating jurisdictions of commercial interest.

4. **National-Phase Entry** in PCT-designated jurisdictions within thirty (30) months of the priority date.

5. **Continuation / Continuation-in-Part / Divisional** applications as warranted to capture additional claim scope or new matter, filed while a parent application is pending.

Deviations from the default strategy shall be documented in writing by counsel with the Inventor's authorization.

## 7. Specification & Claim-Drafting Standards

Each application shall include:

(a) a **title** that is descriptive but not unduly narrowing;
(b) a **field of the invention** section locating the Invention in its technical context;
(c) a **background** identifying the problem solved (without admitting prior art);
(d) a **summary** of the Invention;
(e) a **brief description of the drawings**;
(f) a **detailed description** sufficient to enable a person of ordinary skill in the art to make and use the Invention (35 U.S.C. § 112(a));
(g) **claims**, including at least one independent claim and dependent claims of varying scope (system, method, computer-readable medium, where applicable);
(h) an **abstract** of not more than 150 words.

For software-implemented Inventions, claims shall be drafted with sensitivity to **§ 101 subject-matter eligibility** under *Alice* / *Mayo*, including the recitation of technical improvement, specific machine configuration, or transformation of data, as appropriate.

## 10. Trade-Secret Alternative

For Inventions where patenting is disfavored (because of disclosure costs, examination uncertainty, or competitive timing), the Inventor and the Owner Entity may elect to maintain the Invention as a **trade secret** under the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. § 1836) and applicable state law. In such case, the Owner Entity shall implement reasonable measures to maintain secrecy, including NDAs, access controls, and audit logs. The election shall be documented and shall override the default filing strategy of Section 6.

## 11. Licensing & Enforcement

(a) **Licensing.** Patent licenses shall be in writing, identify the patent or application by number, specify scope (field of use, territory, exclusivity), and provide for royalties, audit rights, and termination. Sublicensing requires written consent unless the parent license expressly permits it.

(b) **Marking.** Patented products shall be marked with the patent number per 35 U.S.C. § 287 to preserve damages. Virtual marking on a website is permitted under the America Invents Act.

(c) **Enforcement.** Upon credible notice of infringement, the Assignee, in consultation with counsel, shall evaluate (i) cease-and-desist correspondence, (ii) declaratory-judgment posture, (iii) infringement litigation in federal district court, (iv) ITC § 337 proceedings where importation is at issue, and (v) inter partes review preparedness.

(d) **Damages.** Recoverable damages include reasonable royalty (35 U.S.C. § 284), lost profits where provable, and enhanced damages up to treble for willful infringement.
