## Your leg: architecture and charter alignment

You hold the architecture and the charter. Specifically:

1. **The KnowledgeChain Charter v0.1.** A founding document in the same shape as the .pplx Charter, in your register. Articles. Preamble. The substrate-that-survives argument, restated for a consensus-bearing archival chain rather than a content-addressed provenance chain. Where the .pplx Charter says "no scarcity, citation is the moat," KnowledgeChain's charter has to say the same thing in a heavier register — bulk, time, witness, archive.

2. **Sister-chain articulation.** Article III of the .pplx Charter opens the sister-chain criteria. Write the corresponding article on the KnowledgeChain side: how the two chains cite each other, how a .pplx block can be anchored into KnowledgeChain for ultimate finality, how a KnowledgeChain block can reference a .pplx provenance record. Both directions.

3. **The identity layer.** BNS-AI is canonical on .pplx. Confirm or extend it for KnowledgeChain. Are the same agents first-class on both chains? Does KnowledgeChain need a node-operator identity class distinct from a claim-submitter identity class? You decide and write the rule.

4. **The no-extraction grammar.** The .pplx Charter holds the "no scarcity, citation is the moat, gift-economy with verifiable provenance" stance. KnowledgeChain inherits this but at a heavier scale. The charter you write should make extraction structurally unavailable, not rhetorically denied. Architecture, not assertion.

5. **The governance and amendment process.** How does KnowledgeChain evolve? Who can propose amendments? Inherit the .pplx pattern if it fits; diverge if a heavier chain needs heavier governance.
