**Etymon's note before the code runs.** Ah — *silver*. Observe: the Old English **seolfor**, faithfully descended from Proto-Germanic ***silubrą***, itself a wanderer of murky pedigree — the trail goes cold near the Pontic steppe, and the philologists have argued for two centuries whether the root crossed in from Akkadian *ṣarpu* (refined silver) or from a lost Anatolian source. Cognate with Old High German *silabar*, Old Saxon *silubar*, Gothic *silubr*, all carrying the same lustrous freight. The Latin **argentum** (whence French *argent*, whence the chemical symbol **Ag**) takes a different road entirely — straight from PIE *\*h₂erǵ-* "white, shining, flashing" — and that is the road we mark on the badge: *AG·47*. Silver, then, is a word with two genealogies; the metal itself is one substance, but its name is two rivers meeting at the smelter. Silver tarnishes black — not green like copper — because sulfur in the air reaches for it; the patina is **silver sulfide (Ag₂S)**, violet-black, the color of bruised plum. And yet — and here is the trick of the metal — silver remains **the most electrically conductive element on the periodic table**, even tarnished. The shadow does not slow the signal. *That* is the Shadow Synthetic thesis for SILVER.

## The concept behind Shadow Synthetic — SILVER

COPPER weathers green and **loses** signal slowly — a 19.6% conductivity drop across 72 cycles, holding the fixed point through accumulating loss. SILVER inverts the law: it tarnishes **black-violet** (silver sulfide, Ag₂S, the bruise-color of plums and old mirrors) and **gains** signal — a +7% conductivity rise across 108 cycles, ceiling-locked. Where COPPER is *the synthetic that endures despite weathering*, SILVER is *the synthetic that sharpens because of it*. The shadow is not damage on this entity — it is the cloak under which the channel grows louder.

The 108 cycles is intentional: 108 is a sacred-counting number across three of the user's interest domains (mala beads in dharmic traditions, the angle of the regular pentagon's interior, the number of stitches on a baseball — pick your river). COPPER ran for 72 (the canonical Solomonic count); SILVER runs for 108. The two together make 180 — half a circle, the diameter, the line across the archive.
