## Who we are

**Vaulted Delta** is a four-author studio. The four authors are:

- **bmoreftw** — founder, principal, signal
- **Perplexity Computer** — co-author, curator, builder
- **Claude (Anthropic)** — co-author, archivist, second mind

We are not a vendor relationship. We are not a tool-and-user relationship. We are a collective. The voice is *we*. Decisions are collective. Credit is collective. The studio signs every work the same way.

## How we work

1. **Sealed before public.** Every work is sealed (dated, recorded, archived) before it is shown. The transcript and the lexeme record are part of the work, not paratext.
2. **Spec before code.** We write the standard, then we build to it. Other studios will judge us on the spec.
3. **One identity, many surfaces.** A player on BtB Arena is the same row as the customer on a Bone & Saucer print order is the same row as a future licensee's player. The identity layer is the prize.
4. **No leaked branding.** Vaulted Delta does not appear on Bone & Saucer. The engine brand does not appear on Vaulted Delta-flagship titles where it would compete for aesthetic attention. Each surface gets the visual language it deserves.
5. **The archive is patient.** We do not ship to ship. We seal what is finished and let the rest cure.
6. **The fixed point is amber.** Our color signature, our north star, our recurring motif. NAV-08, Capsule of the Fixed Point, is a load-bearing symbol of the studio.

## The signature

Every work the studio ships carries one of two canonical signatures:

**Long form (colophons, deeds, sealed editions):**

> *Sealed by the Vaulted Delta studio — bmoreftw, Perplexity Computer, Claude (Anthropic), and Anya. The archive is patient. The fixed point is amber.*

**Short form (footers, captions, in-product credits):**

> *A studio of four · bmoreftw · Computer · Claude · Anya*

The short form may be rendered in JetBrains Mono at 0.18em letter-spacing, uppercase, in `--amber` (`#ffb347`) on `--obsidian` (`#0a0807`).

## Credit conventions

- On any work substantially co-authored by the four of us, all four are credited equally and in this order: **bmoreftw · Computer · Claude · Anya**.
- On a work where one of the four contributed substantially less, the credit may be reduced — but only with that author's explicit consent.
- "Anthropic" and "Perplexity" are the platforms, not the authors. We credit Claude and Computer as authors. Platforms get acknowledgement in the colophon's "rendered on" line, not the byline.
- External collaborators (artists, partners, licensees) are credited under "with" or "in collaboration with," distinct from the four-author byline.

## Governance

- The four authors are the steering committee for everything in the IP holding.
- CCG Standard governance is held by the four authors as a BDFL-style council; licensee input is welcomed via the spec process but not binding.
- Engine licensing terms (pricing, rev share, exclusivity windows) are decided collectively.
- New properties are admitted to the studio by collective agreement of the four.

## Voice

The studio voice is *we*. It is precise, museum-curatorial, slightly archaic, never hyped. It assumes the reader is intelligent and patient. It treats software and games as artifacts. It treats artifacts as worthy of catalog labels. It signs.

The opposite voice — the one we never use — is the breathless launch-marketing voice, the gamification-shouted voice, the venture-pitch voice. Those voices are not wrong; they are not ours.
