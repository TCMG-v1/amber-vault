## Anticipated Cost Categories

These are the categories the ledger will tag entries with. No amounts are committed until they are real, but this is the shape:

- **build** — design, illustration, printing, prototype runs, software licenses for card design
- **hosting** — `pixel-deck.pplx.app` (free under Perplexity Pro), any custom domain renewals
- **fulfillment** — print-on-demand vendor fees, shipping, packaging
- **operations** — payment processor fees (Stripe), tax, accounting if hired

## The Founder's Seed entry — narrative gloss

The first entry on this ledger is not a build cost. It is a marker — $2,500 of prior funding the founder put into the broader work that the Pixel Deck emerges from. That funding kept compute running, kept hardware accreting, kept the substrate alive long enough for the deck to even be designable. The ledger honors it by counting it as Entry 001.

The deck has to earn this back before the split flips. That's not a punishment — it's a *closure*. The founder seeded with personal fiat; the deck repays that seed; *then* the foundations begin to net.

That's the bounded-time / data-dependent shape. The ledger doesn't pretend the seed was free. It also doesn't pretend the seed was a loan. It treats it as what it was: *first investment, first cost, first thing recovered.*

## What "The Foundations" means here

Intentionally vague in v0.1. The beneficiary line reads "The Foundations" because the founder is being spontaneous and hasn't committed to a single entity. When that commitment is made (or when the post-payback flip approaches), this section gets a proper update — formal entity name, mission, public ledger link.

Until then: foundations get 20% pre-payback, 80% post-payback. Disbursement happens at quarterly intervals to whatever foundation entity is designated at that time.
