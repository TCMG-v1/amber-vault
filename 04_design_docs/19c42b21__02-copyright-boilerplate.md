## 1. Recitals & Purpose

This document is the **master copyright boilerplate** for the original works of authorship identified in Schedule A (collectively, the "Works"). It is intended to:

(a) identify each Work, its author, its date of creation, its date of first publication (if any), and the Owner Entity to which copyright has been or shall be assigned;
(b) record the human-authorship facts material to copyrightability under 17 U.S.C. § 102 and current U.S. Copyright Office guidance regarding AI-assisted works;
(c) establish the deposit, registration, and renewal protocol for each Work; and
(d) serve as the canonical reference for license agreements, infringement actions, and assignment recordations.

This boilerplate is read together with the Trademark Boilerplate (Document 01), the Provenance Ledger (Document 02 in the operational stack), and the IP Assignment Agreement (Document 04).

---

## 2. Definitions

- **"Work"** — any original work of authorship identified in Schedule A, including textual, visual, audiovisual, musical, software, and compilation works.
- **"Author"** — the natural person who created the Work or whose human authorial contribution renders the Work copyrightable.
- **"AI-Assisted Work"** — a Work in which artificial-intelligence tools were used in any stage of creation, where the human Author exercised creative selection, arrangement, modification, or curation sufficient to support copyright in the human-authored expression.
- **"Claimant"** — the Owner Entity holding the copyright by assignment.
- **"Deposit Copy"** — the copy of the Work submitted to the U.S. Copyright Office in connection with registration.
- **"Effective Date of Registration"** — the date the Copyright Office receives a complete application, deposit, and fee, per 17 U.S.C. § 410(d).
- **"Provenance Ledger"** — the timestamped file-hash record of authorship and development history maintained per Document 02.

## 4. Authorship Statement & AI-Assistance Disclosure

For each Work, the following authorship facts are affirmed by Claimant as of the Portfolio Effective Date:

1. **Human Author.** Matthew Lansing is the natural-person author of each Work, having exercised the creative judgment, selection, arrangement, and modification that constitute authorship under 17 U.S.C. § 102.

2. **AI-Tool Disclosure.** Where artificial-intelligence tools (including but not limited to large language models, image-generation models, and code-generation models) were used in the creation of a Work, the Author:

   (a) directed the AI tool by means of prompts, parameters, reference materials, and iterative feedback constituting creative choice;
   (b) selected from among AI outputs and rejected those that did not meet the Author's creative vision;
   (c) modified, edited, refined, composited, or otherwise transformed AI outputs into the final Work; and
   (d) integrated AI-assisted elements with original human-authored elements (text, code, sketches, color systems, conceptual frameworks) such that the resulting Work reflects substantial human authorship.

3. **No Derivative-Work Conflict.** No Work is a derivative work of any pre-existing third-party copyrighted work. The AnyaBear character is original to the Author and is **not derived from, based upon, or substantially similar to** any prior character, franchise, or fictional work, including but not limited to *Spy×Family* or any other property featuring a character named "Anya."

4. **Registration Disclosure.** When registering a Work that involved AI assistance, the Claimant shall, in conformity with U.S. Copyright Office guidance (March 16, 2023; subsequent updates), (i) identify the AI-generated material excluded from the copyright claim, (ii) describe the human-authored material claimed, and (iii) provide a clear statement of the Author's creative contribution. The Claimant shall not misrepresent AI-generated material as solely human-authored.

5. **Provenance Evidence.** The Provenance Ledger contains, for each Work, (i) SHA-256 hashes of source files and final Work, (ii) timestamps of major creative milestones, (iii) a record of AI tools used (where applicable), and (iv) the Author's signed affirmation of human authorial contribution.

## 5. Registration Protocol

For each Work that the Claimant determines worth registering, Owner shall:

(a) prepare a complete application on the appropriate form (Form VA for visual; Form TX for textual; Form PA for performing; Form SR for sound; Form CO online for most contemporary applications);
(b) submit a Deposit Copy meeting the regulations at 37 C.F.R. § 202.20;
(c) pay the prevailing filing fee;
(d) accurately disclose AI assistance per Section 4 above;
(e) retain proof of filing, including the Effective Date of Registration; and
(f) record the certificate, once issued, in the Portfolio Register.

**Priority of Registration.** The following Works shall be registered as soon as practicable:

1. **AnyaBear — Canonical Character Design** (visual); Form VA.
2. **AnyaBear — Three Coats Series** (visual compilation); Form VA.
3. **Aggriparium Grammar** (compilation; literary); Form TX.
4. **Spacephrasing Grammars** (literary / software hybrid); Form TX with software-deposit deposit rules per 37 C.F.R. § 202.20(c)(2)(vii).

Registration within three (3) months of first publication preserves the statutory-damages and attorneys'-fees remedies of 17 U.S.C. §§ 412 and 504(c).

## 7. Term

Copyright in each Work subsists for the term provided by 17 U.S.C. § 302, which, for works of corporate or anonymous authorship and works made for hire, is the shorter of ninety-five (95) years from first publication or one hundred twenty (120) years from creation; and for works authored by a natural person and subsequently assigned, the life of the natural-person author plus seventy (70) years.

For Works in which the human Author retains authorship credit and the Claimant holds rights by assignment, the term is computed by reference to the natural-person Author's life unless the Work qualifies as a work made for hire under 17 U.S.C. § 101.

(b) **Non-exclusive licenses** may be oral or implied by conduct but shall, as a matter of policy, be in writing and reference this boilerplate.

(c) **Sublicensing** by licensees is prohibited absent express written consent of Claimant.

(d) **Termination** of pre-1978 grants and grants by individual authors is governed by 17 U.S.C. §§ 203 and 304(c). Calendar reminders shall be set for the 35-year window (assignment by author) and the 56-year window (pre-1978).

(e) **Moral-rights-equivalent provisions.** Although U.S. law provides limited moral-rights protection outside the Visual Artists Rights Act, the Author hereby reserves, to the maximum extent permitted by law and contract, the right to attribution and the right against derogatory treatment of the Works.

## 10. Infringement Enforcement

Upon credible notice of infringement, Claimant shall, in consultation with counsel:

(a) preserve evidence (screenshots, archive captures via the Internet Archive, hash verification);
(b) issue a DMCA notice under 17 U.S.C. § 512(c) where appropriate;
(c) issue a cease-and-desist letter;
(d) where the infringement is willful or commercially significant, file suit in federal district court within the three-year statute of limitations of 17 U.S.C. § 507(b); and
(e) seek statutory damages, actual damages, attorneys' fees, and injunctive relief as appropriate.
